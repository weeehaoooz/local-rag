# News: Breakthrough in Solid-State Battery Technology for Electric Vehicles

**Date**: May 15, 2024
**Source**: Clean Mobility Insights
**Author**: Sarah Jenkins, Senior Electrochemical Engineer

## Summary
In a major advancement for the automotive industry, researchers have successfully demonstrated a high-density solid-state battery capable of doubling the range of traditional lithium-ion batteries while drastically reducing charging times. This breakthrough addresses the primary concerns of electric vehicle (EV) adoption: range anxiety and slow recharging.

## Key Insights
* **Energy Density**: The new solid-state design offers an energy density of over 500 Wh/kg, which is nearly twice that of current liquid-electrolyte batteries.
* **Rapid Charging**: Laboratory tests show that a 10% to 80% charge can be achieved in under 10 minutes without compromising battery longevity.
* **Safety Protocols**: The non-flammable solid electrolyte significantly reduces the risk of thermal runaway, making EVs safer than ever before.
* **Longevity**: Preliminary cycling tests indicate that the battery maintains over 90% capacity after 1,000 charge cycles, potentially outlasting the vehicle itself.

## Corporate and Regional Collaborations
The development is the result of a multi-year partnership between QuantumScape and several European automotive giants, including Volkswagen Group. Regional battery hubs in Germany and the Nordic countries are already beginning to retool their production lines to accommodate the manufacturing of these solid-state cells.

## Future Outlook
As production scales up, this technology is expected to reach cost parity with lithium-ion batteries by 2028. The industry-wide shift toward solid-state power could accelerate the transition to fully electric transport, particularly for heavy-duty trucking and long-distance travel.
